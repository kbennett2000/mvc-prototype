A gentle walk through the first thirty psalms — poems and prayers from the heart of ancient Israel that still speak today. Each psalm takes only a few minutes to read. Sit with the words; let them breathe.

The psalms move across the full range of human experience: praise, lament, fear, gratitude, and quiet trust. Whatever today holds for you, you'll likely find a psalm that meets you there.

**How to use this plan:** Read the psalm slowly — once through for the sense, then again listening for the word or phrase that stays with you. You might jot a note in the margin or a journal. No commentary required; let the text do the work.
