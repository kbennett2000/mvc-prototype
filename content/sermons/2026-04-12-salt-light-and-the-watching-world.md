The sermon, delivered primarily by guest speaker Nathan Smith during a local missions conference at Majestic View Church, centers on the theme of viewing one’s hometown as a vital mission field. Drawing from Jeremiah 29, Smith parallels the Israelites' exile in Babylon to the modern believer’s placement in their community, emphasizing that God has intentionally "sent" and "planted" them where they are to seek the welfare of their neighbors. Smith challenges the congregation to move beyond a "hunker down" mentality of merely protecting their own families, pointing out that in areas like Elbert County, approximately nine out of ten people do not know Jesus. Through personal stories involving his children’s experiences in high school sports, he illustrates how being an "influencer" rather than just being "influenced" by the culture can open doors for discipleship and gospel conversations in everyday settings. The service concludes with a call to obedience to the Great Commission, with Pastor Jonathan noting that true revival in the nation requires a bold commitment to sharing the gospel at home.
