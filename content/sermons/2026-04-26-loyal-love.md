The sermon "Trusting Jehoshua," delivered on April 26, 2026, at Majestic View Church, focuses on Joshua 1:1–18 and the theme of finding victory through absolute surrender to God. Pastor John begins by noting that while the week had been one of his "darkest," it was also one of the "brightest" because of visible symptoms of revival within the church.
The core of the message explores the transition of leadership from Moses to Joshua and the specific call God placed on Joshua's life. A central insight offered is the significance of Joshua’s name; originally named Osha (meaning "salvation"), his name was changed by Moses to Jehoshua (meaning "Yahweh's salvation"), which is the Hebrew name for Jesus. Pastor John uses this connection to draw parallels between trusting the biblical Joshua and trusting Jesus Christ today.
The sermon is structured around three reasons why "Jehoshua" can be trusted:
He was Promised and Called: Just as Joshua was directly commissioned by God to lead Israel across the Jordan to claim their inheritance, Jesus was promised throughout the Old Testament and called to be the Savior of the world.
He Accepted the Challenge to be Strong and Courageous: God commanded Joshua three times to be "strong and of good courage". Pastor John relates this to the strength Jesus displayed during His crucifixion and the courage believers need to face their own "giants," such as fear or discouragement.
The People Demonstrated Trust by Preparing to March: The Israelites responded to Joshua’s leadership by pledging their total obedience and accountability. Pastor John challenges the congregation to move from mere "religion" to a "blank check" surrender, where they are "all in" for God’s work.
To illustrate these points, Pastor John shares personal anecdotes, including receiving an anonymous "Be Strong and Courageous" coin on his desk during a difficult week coaching baseball. He also uses a softball trophy to contrast the deadness of modern "idols" (like money or sports) with the presence of a living, supernatural God.
