# Video: Add a sermon

**Length:** 2 minutes
**Audience:** Editors — church staff who post weekly sermons.
**Companion guide:** [02 Add a sermon](../for-editors/02-add-a-sermon.md)

---

## Open with (10 sec)

[Browser open to the church's homepage.]

**Say:** "Hi — in this video I'll show you how to add a new sermon to your church's website. It takes about two minutes. You'll need your YouTube link, the sermon title, and the Scripture passage."

---

## Steps to record (1 min 30 sec)

### 1. Log in (15 sec)

[Type `/admin/` in the URL bar after the church site name.]
[Click **Login with GitHub**.]

**Say:** "Add `/admin/` to your site's address and click Login with GitHub. If this is your first time logging in, click Authorize when GitHub asks."

### 2. Open Sermons (5 sec)

[Click **Sermons** in the left sidebar.]
[Click the **+ New Sermon** button at the top right.]

**Say:** "Click Sermons on the left, then New Sermon up top."

### 3. Fill in the form (45 sec)

[Type a sermon title in the **Sermon Title** field.]

**Say:** "Type the sermon title — whatever the pastor called it."

[Click the **Date Preached** field, pick a date from the calendar.]

**Say:** "Pick the Sunday it was preached using the date picker."

[Fill in **Speaker**, **Series**, **Scripture Reference**, **Book of the Bible**.]

**Say:** "Speaker is who preached it. Series is what teaching series it's part of. Scripture is the passage, like Ruth 2 verses 1 through 23. Book is just the book name — Ruth — used for filtering."

[Copy the YouTube URL from another browser tab, paste into **YouTube Video ID** field, then trim to just the ID after `v=`.]

**Say:** "For the YouTube ID, find the bit in the URL after `v equals`. That's all you need."

[Click the thumbnail upload, pick an image from the computer.]

**Say:** "Upload a thumbnail if you have one — otherwise the site uses a default."

[Type a one-sentence description in the **Description** field at the bottom.]

**Say:** "A one or two sentence description shows up under the sermon title on the site."

### 4. Save and publish (20 sec)

[Click **Save** at the top.]

**Say:** "Click Save up top. That keeps your work."

[Click **Status: Draft**, choose **Ready for Review**.]
[Click **Publish** → **Publish now**.]

**Say:** "Then change status to Ready for Review, and click Publish. Your sermon will be live within a few minutes after a tech volunteer approves it."

---

## Wrap (10 sec)

[Show the homepage with the new sermon visible.]

**Say:** "And that's it. A written walkthrough with screenshots is linked below. Thanks for watching."

## Things to watch out for

- Don't film with private info on screen (real emails, real names of people not in the staff list).
- Test that audio is balanced (voice louder than browser sounds).
- The mouse cursor should be enlarged for visibility — most screen-record tools have a setting for this.
